# Voice Corpus — Canonical Examples

This page collects canonical examples of the ShayanSpiel voice across platforms. Each entry shows the opening 2 lines, the closing Note: line, and a reference to the full post. New drafts should match the *voice register* of the closest-matching example, not the structure.

---

## 1. Confessional-Teaching (LinkedIn)

**File:** `content/posted/2026-06-08-linkedin-rules-to-loops-v2.md`

> A year ago I built a prompt. 2000 words of rules.
>
> "always check the index. never overwrite existing content. link to at least 2 pages. log every action."

> Note:
> I absolutely wrote the big prompt first. Spent months adding rules to it. The day I switched to the loop was the day the system started working.

**Signature moves:** direct quote as tension, confession arc (tried the wrong thing first -> discovered the right thing), "What nobody tells you" framing, "it is not a X problem. It is a Y problem."

---

## 2. Story-with-Lesson (LinkedIn)

**File:** `content/posted/2026-06-10-linkedin-01-session-as-content.md`

> I ran content calendars for 8 years.
>
> They all had the same arc.

> Note:
> I absolutely built this for myself first. The fact that it became a methodology was an accident. But once you see content as a byproduct instead of a separate job, you cannot go back.

**Signature moves:** "I did X for Y years" opener, short declarative lines, -> arrow as structural pivot, "Here is what I learned the hard way" transition, Note: line that reframes the whole post.

---

## 3. Listicle Counter-Intuitive (LinkedIn -- Optimized)

**File:** `content/posted/2026-06-06-corpus-LI-01-optimized.md`

> Big markets. Big teams. Big budgets. Big failures.
>
> It's not the budget. It's not the team. It's the 10 things on this list -- and the one thing nobody talks about.

**Signature moves:** Anaphora (repeated "Big"), parallel negation ("It's not X. It's not Y."), numbered list with bold hooks, casual "nobody talks about" framing.

---

## 4. Listicle Loose (LinkedIn -- Original)

**File:** `content/posted/2026-06-06-corpus-LI-01-original.md`

> Why Do Most Early-Stage Startups Fail in Marketing?
>
> Everyone talks about budget or bad teams being the cause of failure. But here's what really drives startups into the ground when it comes to marketing...

**Signature moves:** Question-as-title, "Everyone talks about X. But here's what really..." contrast opening, listicle structure (10 reasons), explanatory body per reason.

## 5. Velocity / Numbers (LinkedIn)

**File:** `content/posted/2026-06-08-72-hour-sprint-report.md`

> Three days. Two repos (ShayanWiki + ShayanSpiel.Github.io). One agentic content system. A full landing page. 4 published blog posts. 3 auto-posted LinkedIn pieces. 18 command files. 2 state machines. 5 market-ready offers. ~85 queue drafts.

**Signature moves:** Numbers-as-rhythm opener (paragraph of comma-separated stats), sprint framing (compressed timeline), results-first structure.

## 6. Long-Form Blog (Pillar)

**File:** `content/posted/2026-06-06-pillar-blog.md`

> I do the work. I never post about the work. That was my problem for two years.
>
> Every Tuesday I would finish a build session, look at the work, think "I should post about this," and not post.

**Signature moves:** Personal problem opener in 2 sentences, "I had X for Y years" pattern, concrete details (day of week, action, feeling), the "$5/month" hook in subtitle, build-in-public framing.

## 8. Reader-Problem First (LinkedIn -- perception-shift)

**File:** `content/posted/2026-06-08-linkedin-raw-folder-v2.md`

> Remember the "I will organize this note later" pile?
>
> The notes app graveyard. Browser bookmarks from 2015. Physical notebooks with one page filled.

**Signature moves:** Reader problem as hook (the pile -- not "I had a pile"), rhetorical question, concrete image (graveyard, books from 2015, notebooks with one page), then flips to solution with ->. This is the alternative to the confessional "I used to..." opener.

## How to Use This Corpus

Before drafting, scan the 8 examples above and identify which voice register fits your post:

| If your post is... | Match to example |
|---|---|
| A lesson from a build mistake | #1 (confessional-teaching) |
| A framework / methodology you discovered | #2 (story-with-lesson) |
| A numbered list of insights | #3 or #4 (listicle) |
| A shipping announcement with numbers | #5 (velocity) |
| A deep architecture post | #6 (long-form blog) |
| A one-line hot take | #7 (short-form X) |
| A perception-shift about the reader's pain | #8 (reader-problem first) |

The voice register is the *tone* and *rhythm* -- not the structure. A confessional-teaching post can be 3 paragraphs or 10. Vary your openings: use the reader-problem first (#8) at least as often as the confessional opening (#1-#2).
