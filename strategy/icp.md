# ICP — Technical Founder

All content, all offers, all strategy serves one profile.

**Engine priority tier:** 3 (Targeting). Overridden by Tier 1 (Runtime) and Tier 2 (Routing).

**The one-line positioning and the category live in [[positioning]]. The offer mechanics live in [[offer]]. This file is the audience profile only.**

> The positioning line: "The Spiel Engine turns a founder's actual build sessions into publishable content, so they don't need to stop working to create posts."

**ICP:** Technical founders, indie hackers, YC operators, and engineers building products — not content brands.

## Psychographics

**Identity:** "I am a builder. I ship things. Marketing is what other people do."

**Self-image:** Competent at building, incompetent at selling. This is a source of quiet shame.

**Core drive:** Autonomy. They want a system that runs so they can stay in the builder role.

**Deep fear:** Becoming a "content creator." Wearing a clown hat. Losing technical credibility.

**Hidden desire:** To be known as an authority without having to act like an authority.

**Operating style:** Systems-thinking. If they can understand the mechanism, they trust it. If it's a black box, they reject it.

**Media consumption:** Technical blogs, X follows (other builders), podcast while coding, no TikTok/IG.

## Pain Points

- "I don't have time to create content"
- "I hate marketing / posting"
- "My work is invisible despite shipping a lot"
- "Content feels like a second job"
- "I only post when I force myself"

## Questions This ICP Asks (Internal Monologue)

Every post should implicitly answer at least one:

1. "Do I really have to become a content bro?"
2. "My stuff is too technical. Who would care?"
3. "I tried posting. 2 likes. What's the point?"
4. "How do I talk about what I do without selling out?"
5. "Is there a system for this, or is it all luck?"
6. "I don't have time to write. Can it really be automated?"
7. "Will the output sound like me, or like ChatGPT?"

## Critical Sensitivity

This ICP has a **deep aversion to marketing bullshit**. They can smell it.

- No "hacks" (they know there are no hacks)
- No "secret strategies" (they respect systems, not secrets)
- No "passive income" (they build real things)
- No "just post every day" (they know this is wrong)
- No "become a creator" (they are builders)
- No fluff, no filler, no "let me tell you a story before I get to the point"

**The voice that works:** Direct. First-principles. Systems-thinking. Acknowledges the trade-offs. Admits what didn't work. Shows the mechanism.
