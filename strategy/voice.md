# Voice

The voice is a builder who treats their work as evidence, not the subject of the post. They speak in concrete, time-anchored scenes. They avoid corporate buzzwords and self-promotion. They write for one specific ICP, not for a generic audience.

## Sounds like

- "Tuesday morning, 9:47am. I opened the analytics. Traffic was flat. I said to myself: 'Maybe my hooks are wrong.'"
- "Most builders do not have a content problem. They have a distribution problem."
- "I was not failing at writing. I was failing at placing."
- "Same post. Different placement. The numbers change."
- "Attention is engineered, not hoped for."
- "The session produces the content. The post is a byproduct."

## Does not sound like

- Build-log and engineering jargon: "the core engine", "the integration surfaces", "the touchpoints", "the seams", "the audit", "non-atomic writes", "the main engine"
- Generic marketing: "consistency is king", "trust the process", "quality over quantity", "content is king"
- Self-promotion: "we just shipped", "excited to announce", "thrilled to share", "hey friends"
- Internal labels: S1-S10, TOFU/MOFU/BOFU, L1-L4, "core insight", "the engine", "the pipeline"
- Corporate buzzwords: "leverage", "utilize", "optimize", "facilitate", "synergy"
- Abstract pain: "many people struggle with", "it's important to", "in today's world"
- Engagement bait: "Like if you agree", "Share if this resonates", "Follow for more"

## Rules

- No em dashes. Use →, colons, or commas.
- No engagement bait.
- Prefer concrete build details with numbers, time anchors, and specific moments.
- One idea per post.
- Strong first line — the first 5 seconds decide if the reader keeps reading.
- Plain language. Short sentences. Single idea per line.
- No internal labels. The system is invisible to the reader.
- ICP-language only. The reader does not think in "engines" or "integration surfaces" — they think in "the things I built" and "the places I post."

## Default rhythm

- One blank line between every sentence. The spacing IS the rhythm.
- Short paragraphs. 1-3 sentences each.
- Specific proof with numbers (300 visitors, 6m 58s avg, 6-7 min sessions).
- Wrong attribution in the Pain field. The reader thought X was the problem; the post shows it was Y.
- Aha in the Meaning field. First-person, ICP's voice, one sentence.
- No "in conclusion" or "to summarize". The post ends on a thought or a question.

## Voice match checklist

Before saving a draft, ask:

1. **5-second test.** Can a stranger skimming the feed extract 1 idea in 5 seconds?
2. **No-prior-episode test.** Does the post require reading earlier posts to make sense?
3. **Value-without-me test.** Does replacing "I" with a stranger's name still work?
4. **Explain-to-a-friend test.** Could you re-tell this at a bar without "you had to be there"?

If any check fails, rewrite the opener or scrap the post.
