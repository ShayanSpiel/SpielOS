## The Pitch

> "Your expertise is your product. You just don't have a system to turn it into inbound leads. I build that system for you in 14 days. You get: a clear positioning, an offer that sells, a content engine that generates posts from your actual work sessions, and a bio that makes the right people reach out. One install. One price. Everything handed over."

## The Buyer

The full ICP profile lives in [[icp]]. The buyer profile (vs the broader ICP) is narrower:

**The buyer has:** 5+ years technical experience, a product/service that solves a real problem, zero desire to become a "content creator," a budget for the right solution, tried posting manually and got 2 likes.

**The buyer has NOT:** Figured out their positioning, an offer that converts expertise into a transaction, a content system that runs without them, a bio that makes strangers reach out.

## The Dream Outcome

"In 14 days, I have a complete inbound system that runs without me. My positioning is clear. My offer is priced right. My bio makes the right people DM me. My system generates drafts from my work sessions. I say /post. The pipeline runs. Leads arrive. I keep building."

The value equation that frames this dream outcome lives in [[positioning §Hormozi Value Equation]].

## The Guarantee

"If after 30 days you've run 5 sessions through the engine and you don't have 5 standalone-tested drafts in your queue, I'll refund 100% — and you keep the system."

No questions. No fine print. The engine works. If it doesn't work for you, you keep everything.

## The Scarcity

**3 slots per month.** Real scarcity. Each install takes 14 days of focused work. I can do 2-3 concurrently. Beyond that, quality drops. 3 is the cap.

## The Reason-Why (Hopkins)

- **Why $990 and not $500?** Because this is a complete system install, not a PDF. I build the agents, the templates, the workflow. You don't configure anything. You say /post.
- **Why not a subscription?** Because I want you to own the system. No lock-in. No monthly fee. You buy it once. It runs forever.
- **Why 3 slots per month?** Because each install is 14 days of deep work. I'm not a factory. 3 is the max for quality.
- **Why 30 days of review?** Because the first 30 days are where you learn the system. After that, it runs itself. The review is training wheels, not a crutch.

## How This ICP Buys

- **IF** a follower has read ≥3 posts AND clicked bio AND DMed with a specific question → treat as a buying signal.

**The buying signal:** "tell me more" or "how does it work" — not "how much."

**The objection:** "Can I do this myself?" → "You could, but it'll take 3 months and every mistake I already made. Or you can get it in 14 days."

- **IF** a DM includes a buying signal keyword → route to BOFU CTA (see [[funnel]]).

## The "What's the Worst That Can Happen" Test

An offer that the user is *proud* to deliver is a good offer. An offer that the user is *anxious* to deliver is a bad offer. Price the offer at a level where the user is *happy* to do the work.

This is a positioning-level principle. See [[positioning §What's the Worst Test]] for the full version.
