# Voice & Gates

LLM voice markers + 4-check baseline + 10-gate extended + 8-step Compiler.
After filling in your ICP and voice corpus, the LLM uses this as its style guide.

## Voice Markers (Universal)
1. Lowercase i — intentional, not a typo
2. Absolutely — emphatic agreement
3. Note: — final line pivot
4. Self-deprecation — disarms tension
5. Wait, actually — public correction
6. Here's the thing — frames counter-intuitive
7. Specific number — anchors the post
8. Named reader — "for builders", "for founders"
9. Fast pacing — short lines, no filler

## 4-Check Baseline
1. Is the reader's world the subject? — Not the writer's project.
2. Is the tension in the first 2 lines? — No preamble.
3. Is there a named reader? — "founders", "builders", "you".
4. Is the last line a landing, not a summary? — Thought or ask, not recap.

## 10-Gate Extended
1. One ICP — Every post targets exactly one ICP segment.
2. Problem before solution — Pain is named before the fix.
3. Specificity — No vague claims; numbers or named examples.
4. No architecture leaks — Internal labels stay internal.
5. Reader is the hero — Not the writer, not the tool.
6. Lesson surfaced — Practical takeaway exists.
7. Engagement ask — Ends with a question or specific call.
8. One meaning axis — Drawn from the Compiler's 6 axes.
9. No generic statements — Platitudes fail.
10. Grounded references — Named entities have context.

## 8-Step Compiler
1. Load ICP world
2. Simulate ICP reality
3. Load session as evidence
4. Map session → ICP world
5. Extract 6 meanings (systemic, behavioral, philosophical, contrarian, leverage, human)
6. Select one meaning axis
7. Extract single core insight
8. Generate content
